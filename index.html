About Me

I am a forthcoming PhD student in Political Science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison. I hold dual Master’s degrees from the University of Michigan–Ann Arbor: a Master of International and Regional Studies and a Master of Science in Information (Big Data Analytics track).

My work bridges political science and computational methods — particularly data mining, natural language processing, and data visualization — with applications in comparative politics and Middle East studies.

Research

  • Arab Protest Behavior in Israel’s 2023 Judicial Crisis — Master’s thesis combining survey data, fieldwork, and interrupted time series analysis.
  • Religious Doctrine vs. Institutions in Political Speeches — NLP-based classification using BERT and SHAP explanations on political leaders' speeches.
  • State Reactions to Protest Movements — A developing cross-national event-level dataset with regime-type coding and enforcement tactics.

Projects

  • MSI Capstone Project: Tornado Visualization Dashboard — Built an interactive web app using Streamlit and Altair to visualize U.S. tornado trends.
  • Religious Speech NLP Pipeline — Annotated corpus of doctrine/institution references in political speeches, fine-tuned MiniLM model, and interpretability with SHAP.
