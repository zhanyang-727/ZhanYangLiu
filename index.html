Welcome!

I’m a forthcoming PhD student in Political Science at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, with a background in both regional studies and data science.

About Me

I hold dual Master’s degrees from the University of Michigan–Ann Arbor: a Master of Arts in International and Regional Studies with a specialization in the Middle East and North Africa (MENA), and a Master of Science in Information specializing in Big Data Analytics.

My research bridges political science and computational methods. I’m particularly interested in how data science can help explain complex political behaviors—especially protest participation, state responses to dissent, and ideological polarization. I’ve conducted fieldwork in Israel, built natural language processing pipelines for political speech analysis, and developed interactive dashboards to visualize temporal and spatial patterns in public data.

Methodologically, I work with machine learning (supervised and unsupervised), transformer-based NLP models, and visual analytics platforms such as Streamlit and Altair. My projects are driven by a commitment to both empirical rigor and theoretical relevance—integrating statistical modeling with domain knowledge from comparative politics and Middle Eastern studies.
