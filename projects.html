Religious Framing Classifier in Political Discourse

This project applies NLP techniques to classify religious references in political speech as either emphasizing group identity or personal religiosity. Using a fine-tuned sentence-BERT model and SHAP for interpretability, I built a full pipeline from data annotation and preprocessing to model training and analysis. The approach combines supervised learning with domain knowledge from political science to explore how religious rhetoric is framed in public discourse.

Political Opinion Clustering from Election Data in the Middle East

Applied unsupervised learning (PCA, K-means) to election survey data to uncover clusters in political attitudes among voters in the Middle East. Generated clean visual summaries of each latent group.
